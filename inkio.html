Ink.io

Ink.io is the main project for CareerFoundry's UX Immersion course. It is a mobile tattoo app that was created using Balsamiq, Adobe XD, and Axure. A project brief was provided, but students were given the option to customize their projects. I applied the design thinking process to build Ink.io and was able to delve into UX strategies, best practices, and utilize industry tools throughout this process.

My process

Research

I conducted market analysis to evaluate competitors in the online tattoo community and gathered data on their strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and potential threats to a future tattoo app.

Ideation

Now that I've done research and identified observations, it was time to move forward with creating user personas. This deliverable helped put the user at the forefront of design decisions.

Iteration

After creating the personas and user flows, it was time to start sketching low-fidelity wireframes. After receiving peer feedback on the initial wireframes, I made adjustments to create a mid-fidelity prototype on Balsamiq. Then, I conducted usability testing with 6 users who provided feedback on the mid-fidelity prototypes. The results were sorted using affinity mapping and a rainbow spreadsheet.

Exposition

After multiple usability tests, peer feedback, and iterations, a prototype was produced using Adobe XD. The countless iterations helped me identify a good strategy, so I am happy with the end result.

Conclusion

Designing a tattoo app was challenging, but rewarding. Putting myself in the shoes of the user allowed me to brainstorm and strategize different directions to take Ink.io in. It would've been interesting to add a business strategy to this project. There were a few exercises that discussed defending design decisions to stakeholders, a topic that I would like to explore in more depth.
